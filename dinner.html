Dinner is the last big meal I will have for the day, this is usually an hour to 2 hours before the gym as it gives it enough time to digest and settle. It is usually quite big as it has to last me until breakfast.

Here is an example of a typical dinner for me.

Nutritional Information

Pasta:

Calories: 1231

Protein: 29.3g

Fat: 50.5g

Carbs: 160.5g

Pasta can be a good dinner option for bodybuilders looking to build muscle. Pasta is a great source of complex carbohydrates, which provide a sustained source of energy for your workouts. Additionally, pasta is low in fat and high in fiber, which can help regulate blood sugar levels and promote feelings of fullness. By adding a source of protein, such as chicken or lean beef, to your pasta dish, you can create a well-rounded meal that provides the essential amino acids needed for muscle growth and repair. Just be mindful of portion sizes and opt for whole-grain pasta to maximize the nutrient density of your meal. I will have 200g of pasta with a jar of sauce.

Nutritional Information

Beef:

Calories: 325

Protein: 54.7g

Fat: 10.5g

Carbs: 0g

Adding beef mince to a meal can be an excellent option for bodybuilders looking to build muscle. Beef is a rich source of high-quality protein, containing all the essential amino acids needed for muscle growth and repair. Additionally, beef is a good source of iron, zinc, and vitamin B12, which are important for maintaining overall health and energy levels during exercise. Incorporating beef mince into your meals can provide your body with the necessary nutrients to support muscle growth and recovery. Just be mindful of portion sizes and choose lean cuts of beef to keep the saturated fat content in check. I will have 250g with this meal.
